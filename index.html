About Yi Yang

In China, there is an idiom often used to describe the path to success: 厚积薄发 – if one accumulates numerous experiences over a long time, eventually all dots along the way will be subtly connected to achieve one's full potential. I am currently a first-year graduate majoring in HCI at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. Guided by this idiom, I have spent my life gaining various experiences with dedication, curiosity, and success, which I believe has made me very well prepared for being an excellent UI/UX designer.


Skill Highlights:

Cross-discipline background across Media Production, Journalism and Communications, Photography, Music, Design, Programming, and User Experience.

Rich international educational experiences (4 regions), Near all media platforms working experience.

Superior communication, interpersonal and problem-solving skills, Leadership and Teamwork, Efficient Multitasking, and Teaching skills etc.

I love CREATIVE work

With two majors, Journalism and Digital Media Design, in my undergraduate, I was thirsty to learn the ability of telling an engaging story no matter if it is a series of photos or a 30 minute documentary. Therefore, I have completed various courses and internships, which were incredibly nurturing and enables me to develop various skills, like design, reporting and photography, whilst gaining insights into the ways creative industries works.

Due to this huge passion for media, I received my first M.S. degree in Telecommunications from Indiana University, Bloomington. It was a stepping-stone to, building on my existing abilities and emboldening me to take the leap and make a beautiful transition.

Versatile Young Professional

It takes little more than a cursory glance at the creative industry to note the overwhelming trend toward digital technology. Young professionals capable of coding and programming represent the vanguard of new media industry. With this in mind, I have taken an one-year long programming course in Indiana University, gaining proficiency in multiple programming languages: Python, Java, C#, PHP, HTML/CSS, MySQL.

User Interface (UI) Design is one of the most important parts in HCI design, which focuses on anticipating what users might need to do and ensuring that the interface has elements that are easy to use. UI design also has strong connections to media design, from sharing same design principles to utilizing common software.
